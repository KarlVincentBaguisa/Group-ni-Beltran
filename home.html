Life of Rizal

José Rizal (born June 19, 1861, Calamba, Philippines—died December 30, 1896, Manila) patriot, physician, and man of letters who was an inspiration to the Philippine nationalist movement.

Family Background

The Rizal family was known to be well-off family in Calamba, Laguna and theywere considered as one of the biggest families in those times.

Literary Works

Rizal was a polymath, skilled in both science and the arts. He painted, sketched, and made sculptures and woodcarving. He was a prolific poet, essayist, and novelist whose most famous works were his two novels, Noli Me Tángere (1887) and its sequel, El filibusterismo (1891).

The Rizal Monument

The Rizal Monument is a memorial in Rizal Park in Manila, Philippines built to commemorate the executed Filipino nationalist, José Rizal. A plaque on the pedestal's front reads: "To the memory of José Rizal, patriot and martyr, executed on Bagumbayan Field December 30 1896."

Contribution to Philippine History

Discover the significance of historical sites and educational events in honoring Jose Rizal's legacy and shaping the nation's future.

Honoring the Legacy of Jose Rizal

Every year on December 30, Filipinos come together to commemorate Rizal Day, a national holiday that pays tribute to the life and works of José Rizal. On this day, we remember his selfless sacrifice and unwavering commitment to the Filipino people. As we reflect on his remarkable journey, it is also crucial to celebrate his birthday on June 19, a day that reminds us of his enduring legacy and the spirit of heroism he embodied.

Educational Events and Activities

Inspire young people to embody his principles of justice, nationalism, and love for the country. Promote a deeper understanding of Jose Rizal’s invaluable role in shaping the Philippine nation.

His Novels Awakened Philippine Nationalism

Rizal had been very vocal against the Spanish government, but in a peaceful and progressive manner. For him, “the pen was mightier than the sword.” And through his writings, he exposed the corruption and wrongdoings of government officials as well as the Spanish friars.
